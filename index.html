How a leadership assessment software company designed a tiered wholesale channel program — balancing partner incentives, margin protection, and inventory discipline through pricing architecture alone.

Case background

Assessment Co. is a leadership consulting firm offering a proprietary personality and team dynamics assessment tool used by organizational consultants, executive coaches, and HR leaders. The company sells directly to clients at a standard rate of $900 per assessment (MSRP).

To scale distribution without adding headcount, Assessment Co. designed an Alliance partner program — a tiered wholesale channel allowing certified consulting firms to buy assessments at a wholesale price and resell them independently. The design challenge: how do you incentivize volume distribution while protecting margin, preventing stockpiling, and keeping the model simple enough for a small team to manage?

Primary market research across 920 outreaches and 43 decision-maker conversations validated demand and informed pricing assumptions. The model below reflects the resulting architecture.1

Three design principles behind the model
01
Volume wins, but margin must be protected
Lower wholesale prices at higher volumes grow total gross profit even as per-unit margin compresses. The model only works if volume gains outpace margin loss.
02
Partners keep 100% of consulting fees
Alliance partners are consulting firms — not lead generators. Offering a commission on consulting revenue would conflict with their core business. The wholesale spread is their incentive.
03
Pricing architecture replaces contractual policing
Rather than auditing delivery or enforcing use-it-or-lose-it provisions, an annual platform access fee creates natural holding costs on dormant inventory.

The inventory hedge — why the platform fee matters
$1,095
per year · Year 2+
A partner who stockpiles assessments at the Tier 3 price ($400) and goes dormant pays $1,095/yr to sit on that inventory. After two years of inactivity the carrying cost creates real economic pressure to either distribute or exit — without requiring Assessment Co. to audit delivery or enforce contractual minimums.
Active partners who distribute regularly won't blink at $1,095. One assessment sale at any tier covers it entirely.
Model evaluation
Strengths
Partner spread grows with volume ($400→$500) — the incentive is self-reinforcing without requiring cash commissions
Pricing architecture alone enforces partner activity — no contractual complexity required
Partners keep 100% of consulting fees — the assessment differentiates their practice rather than creating a revenue-sharing conflict
$1,095 platform fee is well below SaaS partner program norms while still creating meaningful holding costs
Gross margin at every tier is consistent with or above the company's blended margin — the channel is not dilutive
Risks & open questions
Tier thresholds must measure assessments delivered to clients, not purchased — the model breaks if partners can front-load at Tier 3 prices
A contractual MSRP floor is required — partners must not sell below $900, or the direct channel becomes structurally uncompetitive
ICP separation (direct vs. alliance clients) must be defined before launch to prevent channel cannibalization
Tier volume thresholds were derived from primary research baselines and should be validated against actual partner profiles before finalizing
